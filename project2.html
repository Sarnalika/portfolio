Fake News Detection System

Objective

The project aimed to build a **fake news detection system** using deep learning, specifically the **LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) model**. The goal was to train a model that could accurately classify news articles as **fake or real**, helping to combat misinformation.

Problem Statement

With the rise of social media, **misinformation spreads rapidly**, impacting politics, health, and global events. Traditional rule-based detection methods are ineffective, necessitating **AI-driven solutions** to detect fake news in real-time.

Dataset

The dataset consists of thousands of **real and fake news articles**, collected from various news sources. The dataset includes **textual features** such as headlines, article content, and sources. Preprocessing steps included tokenization, stopword removal, and vectorization.

Approach

  • Preprocessed text data using **NLTK** and **TensorFlow/Keras**.
  • Converted text into numerical representation using **Word Embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe)**.
  • Trained an **LSTM-based deep learning model** for sequence learning.
  • Optimized model parameters for **higher accuracy**.
  • Deployed a **web-based interface** for real-time news classification.

Outcome

The LSTM model achieved an accuracy of **~98%**, significantly improving fake news detection. The model can efficiently classify news articles and can be integrated into **news verification platforms**.
